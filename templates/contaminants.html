Common Water Contaminants

Water contaminants can come from a variety of sources and have a range of effects on human health and the environment. Below are some of the most common contaminants found in water supplies:

1. Bacteria and Viruses

Microbial contaminants such as bacteria, viruses, and parasites can cause severe illnesses. Common sources include sewage overflow, animal waste, and untreated water sources.

  • E. coli: Found in the intestines of animals and humans, can cause gastrointestinal illness.
  • Giardia: A parasite that can cause diarrhea and other health issues.

2. Nitrates

Nitrates are commonly found in fertilizers and can seep into groundwater. High levels of nitrates in drinking water can cause serious health issues, particularly for infants.

  • Sources: Agricultural runoff, septic systems, and industrial waste.
  • Effects: Can lead to methemoglobinemia, or "blue baby syndrome," in infants.

3. Heavy Metals

Heavy metals like lead, mercury, and arsenic can contaminate water through natural deposits, industrial discharges, and old plumbing systems.

  • Lead: Often found in old pipes and plumbing fixtures; can cause developmental issues in children and kidney problems in adults.
  • Mercury: Industrial discharges can lead to mercury contamination, which can affect the nervous system.
  • Arsenic: Naturally occurring in some groundwater; long-term exposure can lead to skin conditions and increased cancer risk.

4. Pesticides and Herbicides

Chemicals used in agriculture and landscaping can runoff into water supplies, potentially causing health issues with long-term exposure.

  • Sources: Agricultural runoff, improper disposal of chemicals.
  • Effects: Can affect the nervous system, hormone systems, and increase cancer risk.

5. Organic Chemicals

These include a wide range of compounds such as solvents, paints, and pharmaceuticals. They can enter water supplies through industrial discharges, landfills, and improper disposal.

  • Sources: Industrial processes, improper disposal of household chemicals, landfills.
  • Effects: Can cause liver, kidney, and central nervous system problems.
